# The Empirical Regulatory Operator (z-score, Option-1 pilot)

One matrix, four questions. The **operator** is the effect of every regulator knockdown on every measured gene — the object the fingerprint PCA computed and half-discarded. We build it as a 3-way tensor `T[regulator, gene, condition]` in **precision-decoupled z-score space** over an expanded **~800-regulator** panel, then ask: what are its gene programs, is condition-gating real, is it predictive, and are its programs donor-reproducible.

Reproduce: `make operator` (Step 0 fetch is one-time then cached; Steps 1–4 offline). Kernels are unit-tested (`tests/test_opkernels.py`, 16 tests).

## Representation, and why z-score (Step 0)

Raw log-FC row-norm is **negatively** correlated with statistical power: `spearman(‖row‖, n_cells_target) = −0.683` on significant rows. Norm tracks noise, not signal — so an SVD/CP on raw log-FC would fit heteroscedastic noise first, exactly where the leading factors (the programs) live. The remote `layers/zscore` is a per-(perturbation, gene) z-score (effect/SE), precision-decoupled and pooled across conditions.

**Tensor:** 800 regulators × 2000 genes × 3 conditions (Rest, Stim8hr, Stim48hr). The 800 are the regulators with the broadest downstream footprints (top by `n_downstream`) **above a median cell-count floor** — 733 of them outside the original 200-regulator fingerprint panel.

- **Selection matters.** Ranking the expansion by `ontarget_effect_size` (an earlier draft) reintroduces the power confound (that ranking preferentially selects low-cell / high-z perturbations → z-space confound ρ = −0.27). Breadth (`n_downstream`) above a power floor decorrelates it: the built tensor has **confound ρ = +0.080** (guard requires |ρ| < 0.15). The floor is essential — breadth-ranking *without* it still leaves ρ ≈ −0.36.
- **One fail-closed guard.** Only the confound guard (|ρ| < 0.15) is asserted (the driver refuses to cache otherwise). Two earlier magnitude-based guards (row-norm CV, per-anchor cross-condition spread) were **retired after verification**: on a per-cell-z, breadth-homogeneous panel, *any* magnitude statistic is nearly invariant to the pooled-vs-within-condition distinction. Row-norm CV (0.229 here) is driven by selection homogeneity, not within-condition z — proven in raw space, where no z exists (this selection's raw row-norm CV 0.28 < a random panel's 0.34). Pooling instead rests on a documented evidence chain: (1) it is a **layer-level** property established on the same `layers/zscore` slice (row-norm CV 0.36 on the heterogeneous 200-panel, before any selection); (2) the confound guard passes; (3) within-condition z would show up downstream as **all-constitutive** CP condition factors — which the Step-2 gating test would expose. That downstream test, not a Step-0 magnitude proxy, is the authoritative pooling check.

## Gene programs (Step 1)

Right singular vectors of the pooled fingerprint matrix, oriented by an ISG anchor, both raw and varimax-rotated, enriched offline (hypergeometric + BH-FDR).

- **The power fix worked.** Every one of the top-10 program's left factors is power-clean: `max |spearman(u_k, n_cells)| = 0.121`, none flagged confounded. In raw-log-FC space the leading programs would risk being power axes; in z-space they are not. This is the clean positive that validates the whole representation choice.
- **Top programs are the expected immune ones.** PC1 = interferon (IFN_ISG, FDR = 1.6e-4, clean). PC2 = TCR-proximal (FDR = 0.063, near-significant).
- **Honest limit:** only **1 of the top-5** PCs clears the strict "FDR < 0.05 label AND power-clean" bar. This is a **coverage limit of the deliberately-minimal 4-set offline enrichment reference** (~11–17 gene sets, 2% tail), not a failure of the SVD programs — the programs recovered are exactly interferon and TCR-proximal as predicted. A richer offline `.gmt` (MSigDB Hallmark/Reactome dropped into `data/genesets/`) would give the enrichment fair power; `load_genesets()` already merges any present.

## Condition gating (Step 2) — flagship of the descriptive half, and the pooling proof

Masked CP (`regulator ⊗ gene ⊗ condition`) with RMS per-condition scale control, split-half stability rank selection, per-factor power/degeneracy/collinearity gates, and a **bootstrap CI on each condition factor**. Gating is `gated_ci=True` only if the CI excludes flat.

- **Pilot config:** rank sweep to 6, stability-subsample 250, 50 bootstrap resamples (the full defaults — sweep to 8, 100 resamples — are the Makefile default / an Option-3 cluster run). Confound meter on the tensor = 0.080.
- **The pooling proof HOLDS.** Three **clean** factors (not power-confounded, not degenerate, `max_cofactor_cosine` < 0.7) are `gated_ci=True`, peaked on Stim48hr (factor 2), Stim8hr (factor 3), and Rest (factor 4). Because within-condition-normalized data cannot produce gating (every slab would be independently scaled → all-constitutive), **≥1 clean gated factor is positive proof the representation is genuinely pooled** — this is the load-bearing check that replaced Step 0's retired magnitude guards, and it passes decisively.
- **Reconciliation with the prior permutation result.** An earlier within-condition permutation found TCR's *complex-cohesion z* was not condition-inflated in z-space (11.2 → 11.2). That is a **different quantity** from CP *program-magnitude* gating across conditions: cohesion of a complex's fingerprints vs the magnitude of a program's transcriptional response by condition. Both can hold at once — they do not contradict.
- **Honest limits (three).** (1) **All factors are `unlabeled`** — the 4-set enrichment panel cannot name them (same coverage limit as Step 1), so we have *proven* gating but *unnamed* programs; the "gated TCR vs constitutive chromatin" biological headline is not cleanly supported by naming here. (2) **No clean constitutive factor** — the constitutive factors (1/5/6) form a collinear cluster (`gene_mode_cosine` ≈ 0.8), so they fail the cleanliness filter. (3) Stability stays > 0.7 across all ranks and *peaks* at rank 3 (0.937), so the "largest rank clearing threshold" rule lands at the ceiling (rank 6); the operator's effective rank is more honestly ~3 by the stability peak.

## Prediction (Step 3) — flagship of the predictive half

3b (out-of-panel condition extrapolation) is the headline; 3a (entry-wise) is a sanity check. Train-only standardization throughout (no leakage).

- **3b beats persistence, cleanly, out-of-panel.** Holding out entire (regulator, Stim48hr) fibers for **160 regulators, 100% outside the original panel**, and predicting them from Rest + Stim8hr via the low-rank fit on other regulators: the low-rank model **beats persistence at every rank 1–12** — the result does not hinge on a lucky rank pick. Model R² rises from 0.028 (rank 1) to **0.154 (rank 11)**, versus a **persistence** baseline (Stim48hr := Stim8hr) of **R² = −0.176**. Persistence is negative — late stim genuinely diverges from early stim — so low-rank structure is really predicting unseen late-stim responses for regulators never characterized. **This converts the operator from descriptive to predictive.** No genome-scale-imputation overclaim: pure low-rank cannot predict a regulator with zero observed entries; 3b holds out a condition *fiber*, never a whole regulator.
- **3a (sanity):** low-rank beats a rank-1 baseline at every rank ≥ 2; R² is still rising at rank 12 (elbow right-censored — a wider sweep would locate it). Beating the per-gene-mean baseline is near-trivial and is *not* claimed.
- **One disclosure:** the 2000-gene axis is selected once (top variance) on the *full* tensor, so 3b's held-out fibers live on a gene set chosen with those rows present. This is a diffuse feature-selection-on-full-data effect, **not** target leakage — standardization and the low-rank fit never read held-out *values* (verified: corrupting held-out entries leaves predictions bit-identical). It does not invalidate the 3b result, but a fully airtight version would re-select genes on train rows only.

## Donor reproducibility of programs (Step 4)

Principal angles between top-k gene-program subspaces across **disjoint** donor pairs only — `(1,2)vs(3,4)`, `(1,3)vs(2,4)`, `(1,4)vs(2,3)` — never the 15 inflated overlapping comparisons (the 6 modalities share donors). **Data not local:** the per-donor-pair matrices are not in the cache (only a `donor_obs.csv` summary), so the driver correctly prints `[NEEDS-DATA]`, writes an empty-headed table, and exits 0. Producing them needs a fetch of the per-donor layers analogous to `scripts/fetch_fingerprint_matrix.py`; the honest cross-donor result is then a one-command run.

## Escalation trigger

**Step 3b beat persistence cleanly on out-of-panel regulators.** Per the pilot's pre-registered trigger, that is the evidence to escalate to **Option 3**: the full 6209-regulator axis, completion as the flagship, and the CP stability sweep + bootstrap on cluster compute (the pilot ran a reduced config to fit local runtime). Option 1 was the pilot that earned Option 3 — decide on this evidence, not by relitigating scope.

**Two curve-driven requirements for the 6209 run** (the pilot R² had **not** plateaued at rank 11 — it was still rising at the sweep ceiling): (1) sweep rank **well past 12** to actually locate saturation, otherwise any "effective rank" number is right-censored and unsupported; (2) frame the headline as **predictivity, not compressibility** — the honest claim is "low-rank structure predicts unseen late-stim out-of-panel," not "the operator is rank-k." The curve does not yet support a low-dimensionality claim, and configuring the cluster sweep without (1) risks quietly overclaiming it.

### 6209 runbook

**(a) "Full axis" is a size to be found empirically, not 3106.** The floor (n_cells ≥ 567) is necessary but **not sufficient**: above it, the breadth-ranked confound still grows monotonically with axis size. On the panel's top-2000 genes (the guard's own pooled metric), raw `ρ(‖slab‖, n_cells)` is **−0.05 at N=800, −0.16 at 1600, −0.22 at 2500, and −0.24 across all 3106 above-floor** (the literal full 6209 is −0.53 on panel genes, −0.66 on all 10282). Only **N=800 is verified guard-clean** — the sole size with a *measured z-space* confound (**+0.08**); for every larger size only the raw number exists, and the raw→z map is unreliable (at 800, raw −0.05 flipped to z **+0.08**), so the raw trend marks larger axes as *riskier*, not safe. The floor keeps exactly **3106 regulators (50.0% of 6209)**, but **3106 is an upper bound on regulator count, not a verified-clean axis size.** The escalation must sweep axis size upward, re-fetch z-scores, and re-assert the confound guard (**|ρ|<0.15**) on each candidate, taking the largest that passes — or add per-cell precision weighting with real `lfcSE` (not local; needs the per-cell layers) to include the low-power tail directly. Decide sweep-to-max-clean-size vs lfcSE-weighting *before* the fetch: it sets both the fetch cost and the headline ("N-regulator operator, N found empirically" vs "6209 with precision weighting").

**(b) Starting config (drivers are escalation-ready — CLI knobs only, no code change):**
- `python scripts/build_operator_tensor.py --n-total 1200 --top-genes 2000` (rebuild the tensor at the larger panel; re-fetches z-score once to `data/cache/panel_zscore_<hash>.npy`, writes `data/cache/operator_tensor.npz` after the confound guard)
- `python scripts/operator_completion.py --max-rank 30 --holdout 0.2` (push past 12 to find the plateau; raise further if R² still climbs at 30 → writes `docs/tables/operator_completion_condition.csv`)
- `python scripts/decompose_operator_cp.py --rank auto --scale-control rms --max-rank 12 --stab-subsample 800 --boot-n 100` (dial the reduced-pilot knobs — pilot ran subsample 250 / 50 boots — up to full config)
- `python scripts/decompose_operator_svd.py --k 10 --tail-pct 2 --rotate` (re-run Step 1 on the new tensor; cheap)

**(c) The plateau check gates any effective-rank claim.** An effective-rank number is only reportable if the 3b R²-vs-rank curve actually saturates on the escalated (~1200–1600) axis. No plateau ⇒ report "predictive, not low-dimensional" and do **not** cite an effective rank. This is the one place the cluster run can silently overclaim, so it is the gate on the headline.

## What the nuisance controls bought us

| Control | Naive version would have… |
|---|---|
| **z-score representation** | fit the −0.68 power axis first; leading "programs" would be noise magnitude. (Power gate: max \|ρ\|=0.121, clean.) |
| **breadth + power-floor selection** | (effect-size ranking) re-imported the confound at z-ρ = −0.27; the guard caught it. |
| **confound guard, retired magnitude guards** | either a false block (row-norm CV on a homogeneous panel) or a magnitude proxy that can't see the real failure mode. |
| **RMS condition scale control** | (no control) made *every* factor trivially "gated" by Stim's larger magnitude. |
| **bootstrap-CI gating** | called gating off 3 numbers with no error bars — one resample from flipping. |
| **train-only standardization** | leaked test statistics into the fit → inflated, invalid R². |
| **persistence / rank-1 baselines** | "beating zero" — meaningless; the honest bars are persistence and rank-1. |
| **disjoint donor pairs** | 15 inflated overlapping comparisons instead of 3 honest ones. |
| **gauge fixing + varimax (true, not quartimax)** | sign/scale/rotation ambiguity read as biology. |

## Escalation to 3106 (Option A, executed)

Reproduce: rebuild `operator-tensor` at `--n-total 3106`, then the pipeline. Escalation outputs are suffixed `_3106` (the `.csv`/`.png` without a suffix remain the 800-pilot record — the escalation is additive, never destructive, so the pilot↔3106 comparison that *is* the result stays verifiable).

**The axis scales 4× clean, and the predictive advantage generalizes — measured, not projected.** Sweeping `--n-total` with the confound guard as arbiter, the guard-clean ceiling turned out to be the **entire above-floor set — 3106 regulators** (confound ρ = **−0.006**; the z-space confound *improved* as the panel grew — +0.08 at 800 → +0.030 at 2000 → +0.012 at 2500 → −0.006 at 3106 — refuting the raw-space expectation that 3106 would fail). The power gate stayed clean at 4× (max |ρ| = **0.049**, no PC confounded): the larger, weaker-tail panel did not reintroduce power.

**Prediction (3b), the flagship, holds against the pre-registered bar.** On 621 held-out out-of-panel regulators, the low-rank model beats persistence at all 50 ranks, **though the model's own R² peaks at rank 7 and declines thereafter** (overfitting) — a robust *advantage* over baseline, on genuinely low-rank structure. The aggregate margin (model − persistence) is **+0.379 @ rank 7**. Strength-stratification (reproducible source: `docs/tables/operator_completion_stratified_3106.csv`) shows the margin *decreases* monotonically with regulator strength: **weak +0.501, strong (median split) +0.277, strong panel-matched +0.205** — because persistence is a progressively fairer baseline for stronger regulators (−0.208 median-split, −0.146 panel-matched) and collapses for weak ones (−0.421). The honest reading is therefore the reverse of an earlier inline claim: the aggregate margin is, if anything, concentrated where persistence is weak, **not** matched to the pilot's +0.330 on the strong stratum. What holds robustly is that **the model beats persistence at every rank in every stratum** (min margins: strong +0.217, panel-matched +0.151, weak +0.424), and the margin is **stable to the holdout draw** — 20 independent random holdouts give aggregate **+0.382 ± 0.011**, weak +0.514 ± 0.015, strong +0.277 ± 0.018, panel-matched +0.206 ± 0.021 (`docs/tables/operator_completion_multiseed_3106.csv`) — so the out-of-panel predictive win is not an artifact of weak-regulator baseline collapse or of a single lucky seed, even though it is larger for weak regulators. *(A prior version cited a "+0.332 ≈ pilot +0.330, apples-to-apples" figure computed inline and never persisted; it does not reproduce from disk and is retired in favor of the stratified numbers above.)*

**And the absolute precision is modest, and softens at scale — we report it.** The model's absolute R² on the held-out out-of-panel fibers is low across all strata: **aggregate 0.074, strong 0.069, panel-matched 0.058, weak 0.080** (source: `operator_completion_stratified_3106.csv`), versus the pilot's 0.154 on its smaller 160-regulator holdout. So the larger, more heterogeneous 3106 population makes the shared low-rank structure a modestly worse *per-regulator* predictor in absolute terms, even for strong regulators. **The beats-persistence advantage is robust and reproducible across strata and ranks; the absolute predictive precision is modest (R² ≈ 0.06–0.08) and does not match the pilot.** Reporting both halves is what keeps this research-worthy rather than an inflated headline.

**The out-of-panel predictive structure is effectively low-rank (~7) at this scale.** The 3b curve peaks at rank 7 and declines through rank 50 — a genuine turnover, not the pilot's right-censored "still climbing" (rank-11, sweep cut at 12). This licenses a low-dimensionality claim **for the ~7 components that predict unseen regulators — not the full operator's rank** — and it is a property of this 3106/holdout configuration, **not an intrinsic constant** (a 6209 run may move it).

**Block A — the predictive rank, cross-validated (not a single seed).** Replacing the single seed-0 holdout with a cross-validated condition-extrapolation (fold over out-of-panel regulators × held-out condition, 15 seeds; `operator_bcv_rank_3106.csv`), the predictive rank for the flagship late-stimulation (Stim48hr) extrapolation is **7 [95% CI 7–7]**, robust to the holdout fraction (rank 7 at 10/20/40%). It is honestly **target-condition-specific**: predicting the earlier conditions instead uses rank 11 (Rest) / 12 (Stim8hr), and those are *easier* (R² ≈ 0.13 vs ≈ 0.06 for late-stim) — late stimulation is the most divergent and hence the hardest, lowest-rank target. The decisive contrast is with the RMT guardrail (Task A): the correlation carries **≈ 92 signal directions** above the empirical noise edge (2.95), but only **~7–12 generalise across the condition split**. Only ~10% of the reproducible signal predicts unseen regulators; the rest is regulator-idiosyncratic — which honestly frames the modest R² ≈ 0.06–0.13 ceiling as **mostly irreducible**, a property of the data, not a failure of the model to fit.

**CP re-confirms pooling at 3106.** Even at reduced config (`--stab-subsample 250 --boot-n 50`), **2 clean factors are gated with bootstrap CIs excluding flat** (peaks Rest and Stim8hr) and **2 clean factors are constitutive** — positive proof the representation stays genuinely pooled at 4× the axis (cleaner than the pilot, which had no clean constitutive factor). The full-config bootstrap is deferred to cluster but was not needed for the re-confirmation; a reduced-config CP has less bootstrap power, so a null there would not be evidence against pooling — here it is not null, it re-confirms.

**Net (the honest headline):** *the predictive advantage over baseline generalizes to 4× the regulator axis and is low-rank (~7)* — with representation clean (ρ=−0.006), power-gate holding (0.049), pooling re-confirmed, and the explicit caveat that absolute predictive precision softens at scale. **Not** "predicts the genome." Per the escalation trigger, this clean out-of-panel result is the evidence that would justify Option B (the `lfcSE` sub-floor build). Option A covers the whole axis *above the floor*, and the floor is well-motivated: a 5-min check confirms the below-floor tail it drops carries the **power-inflated-breadth signature** — *higher* apparent breadth (median n_downstream 8 vs 3; 66% exceed the kept median) but *weaker* on-target knockdown (|effect| 8.1 vs 11.2) at roughly half the cell count — i.e. noise read as signal, not clean large effects. (Those figures are over the ~6,209 regulators significant in all three conditions — the population the panel ranks — split at the n_cells floor into the 3,106 kept above-floor and 3,103 dropped below-floor; a coarser cut over all significant rows gives 2 vs 3 / 56%, same direction.) **But** weak KD is not absent effect: that tail could still hide large-but-noisy real regulators that only an `lfcSE` precision weight could separate from variance — exactly the failure mode flagged in review. So **Option B is not retired by Option A's success; it remains the open escalation** to recover the sub-floor tail, conditional on that low-power tail being the specific target.

*Tooling: `soft_impute` was switched to a truncated-SVD (`svds` top-k) kernel — identical to full-SVD truncation to machine precision (pinned by a 1e-13 equivalence regression test, `test_soft_impute_matches_full_svd_reference`), ~10× faster — which made the rank-50 sweep and the 6209 escalation tractable.*


## Cis-off-target gate audit (Step 1 insurance)

Before building further descriptive cards on top of the ranking and the operator, we confirmed — from the code and the data — what the two paper-feeding analytics actually do with off-target contrasts, and measured the sensitivity to excluding them. Reproduce: `python scripts/audit_offtarget_gate.py` → `docs/tables/offtarget_gate_sensitivity.csv`. Additive throughout: the shipped ranking and the merged-3106 tensor are untouched; the leave-out tensor is a separate gitignored `operator_tensor_cisclean.npz`.

**The flag is cis, and "exclude" is therefore legitimate — proven, not assumed.** `DE_stats.offtarget_flag` matches the tensor obs's `neighboring_gene_KD` at **99.4%**; of flagged rows **92% are cis** (guide knocked down a *neighbouring* gene), 1% distal. Excluding it removes exactly the "cis read as trans" artifact. It covers **11.6%** of the significant rows.

**The ranking was never undefended — off-target is handled three ways.** `model_hubs.py` enters it as a **covariate** (`off_i` in `n_downstream ~ C(culture_condition) + sig_i + off_i`), **annotates** it (`any_offtarget` / "possible off-target"), and **penalises** it in `robust_score` (×0.6 if flagged). The row filter is `ontarget_significant` only, so flagged rows are not *excluded* — but they are covariate-adjusted, flagged, and down-weighted. A hard exclusion leaves the global structure intact: Spearman rank correlation **0.9897**, top-30 overlap **25/30**. What moves is a handful of *individuals* whose height came partly from a neighbouring-gene knockdown (TADA2B #2→#93, TAF6L #9→#130, ELOF1/ATAD5/SUPT7L out of the top-30).

**The operator tensor was the one genuine exposure — and it is robust.** `build_operator_tensor.py` gates on `ontarget_significant_bool` only; it does not reference the cis/distal/low-target flags the obs cache carries. An offline regulator leave-out (drop the **404/3106** regulators with any cis-flagged significant row — no new z-scores fetched — and re-run flagship 3b) leaves the predictive result essentially unchanged: the margin over persistence holds (base **+0.379** @rank 7 → cis-clean **+0.390** @rank 6), beats persistence at every rank 1–12 in both, the low-rank elbow stays **~6–7**, and the slab-matrix effective rank is unchanged (**86.5 → 87.1**). **The leave-out changes the out-of-panel test population (621 → 540 held-out regulators), so the small margin delta is *not* interpretable as an improvement** — the defensible claim is robustness (no degradation), not that cleaning helps.

**SAGA survives, but its leading-subunit list is corrected — and that is the audit working, not failing.** The cis gate discriminates *within* the complex: the cis-clean subunits rise (SUPT20H #4→#3, TADA1 #5→#4, USP22 #27→#22) while the cis-driven ones collapse (TADA2B #2→#93, TAF6L #9→#130, SUPT7L #20→out). In the operator panel, 3/6 subunits (SUPT20H, TAF6L, USP22) anchor the program under the clean gate. The SAGA *identity* is robust to cis exclusion; the specific leading subunits are not the same ones — TADA2B's previous prominence (rank 2) was partly neighbouring-gene off-target. We report the correction rather than bury it.


## Naming the gated CP programs (Step 3)

The condition-gated CP factors pass the pooling test (real, bootstrap-CI gated) but shipped as `program_label = unlabeled` in `operator_cp_factors_3106.csv`. Here we name the two clean gated factors from the **regulator side** (primary), corroborate downstream (secondary), and label honestly where the membership is mixed. Deliverable: `docs/tables/operator_cp_factors_3106_labeled.csv`.

**Non-circularity (state it up front).** CP assigns each regulator to a factor by its loading `a_k`, computed from the z-score tensor **blind to any biological annotation**. Asking whether a factor's high-`a_k` regulators cohere by known function is therefore a genuine test, not a definition — the annotation was never an input. Every regulator-side name anchor here must pass **two independent filters**, both stated explicitly below: **(a) non-circular** — the factor→regulator assignment came from the loading `a_k`, blind to biological annotation; and **(b) cis-clean** — the anchor may rest only on regulators that *survive* the Step-1 cis-off-target gate (e.g. SUPT20H/TADA1/USP22), never on cis-inflated ones (TAF6L/TADA2B), whose apparent strength came partly from neighbouring-gene knockdown. The two degenerate factors (2/5, `max_cofactor_cosine = 0.966`) are excluded; 3/4 are constitutive(flat). The nameable gated factors are 1 (peak Rest) and 6 (peak Stim8hr).

**Factor 6 — earned: early T-cell activation / TCR-signaling *coupled with* SAGA (Stim8hr-gated).** The label rests on two axes that are independent of the regulator labels: (1) the factor peaks at **Stim8hr** (early activation, gated CI), and (2) its downstream genes are enriched for the **empirical** TCR-signaling program markers (`fingerprint_program_markers.csv`, "TCR signaling|up"): 3/10 top genes — GPSM3, LIMD2, MYL12A — hypergeometric **p = 1.9e-5**, with the rest (CORO1A, ARPC1B, MAP4K1/HPK1, SAMHD1, CNN2) being leukocyte actin/T-signaling effectors; the same gene program is recovered as SVD raw-PC3 (independent decomposition). Note the *curated* proximal set `TCR_PROXIMAL` (ZAP70/LCK/LAT) shows **zero** overlap — expected and non-contradictory, because the DE-readout genes are downstream effectors, not proximal kinases. The label is explicitly a **coupling of two things**, not a claim that SAGA *is* activation: the regulators mix early-activation drivers (CD28 costimulation, SLC3A2/CD98hc) with a SAGA chromatin core. Per the Step-1 cis audit, that SAGA core is anchored on the *one* **cis-clean** subunit present — **SUPT20H** (rose #4→#3 under the cis gate) — and explicitly **not** on TADA2B (#2→#93) or **TAF6L** (#9→#130), *both* of which are cis-inflated by neighbouring-gene knockdown (panel-survival is a weaker criterion than the ranking gate and does not rescue them as anchors). Stated honestly, this makes factor 6's SAGA signal **thinner** than its raw subunit list (TADA2B/TAF6L/SUPT20H) implies: cis-clean, it rests on essentially one subunit.

**Factor 1 — earned downstream name, multi-module regulators: Rest-gated proteostasis / RNA-processing.** The downstream side names cleanly by gene identity: chaperonin/mitochondrial proteostasis (**HSPD1 = HSP60, HSPE1 = HSP10, TCP1 = CCT, PRDX3**) plus hnRNP RNA-processing (HNRNPA2B1, HNRNPAB, LSM4, ELAVL1), independently recovered as the bottom tail of SVD raw-PC1 (5/10 genes) — the resting pole of the dominant axis. (There is no curated proteostasis gene-set in-repo, so this is an identity + cross-decomposition call, not a hypergeometric — stated plainly rather than dressed as a p-value.) The **regulator** side is genuinely multi-module: two coherent, literature-anchored modules co-load on this one Rest-gated factor — **AHR–ARNT** (the bHLH-PAS xenobiotic/hypoxia heterodimer; ARNT = HIF-1β) and a complete **immune Rac→WAVE→actin axis** (NCKAP1L = Hem1, the hematopoietic paralog of the WAVE-complex Nap1 subunit; WASF2 = WAVE core; DOCK2 = Rac GEF; ARHGAP30 = Rac GAP). Neither maps to the repo's SAGA/TCR/Mediator annotation. Whether these are one coupled Rest-state program or two programs fused by the rank-6 cut is *probed* by a rank test (re-running CP at ranks 8 and 10), and the evidence is **suggestive of two programs rather than one**: at rank 6 AHR–ARNT and the WAVE-Rac module co-load on one factor, but at ranks 8 *and* 10 they consistently fall on distinct factors — AHR–ARNT alone, and DOCK2/NCKAP1L/WASF2/ARHGAP30 re-cohering as their own actin factor (cleanly at rank 10). This is **consistent with** factor 1 being two programs fused by the rank-6 truncation rather than one genuinely coupled program — but it is **not decisive**, because ranks 8–10 sit **below the CP stability threshold** (the 3106 split-half matched cosine is already 0.715 at rank 6, at the 0.70 bar and declining, so those higher-rank factors are < 0.72 and not individually reproducible). In that unstable regime we cannot distinguish "two real programs emerging at higher rank" from ordinary factor rotation, so we report it as **suggestive, not settled**.

**Step 2 (K562) readiness note.** Checkpoint A (viability) passed by a wide margin — the external Replogle-2022 K562 GWPS shares **2,487** perturbed regulators with the 3106 operator panel (6,301–6,408 with the sig-KD set), far above the ≥100 gate. Harmonization caveats to carry into any Step-2 execution: K562 is CRISPRi in a **cancer line** vs our CRISPRi in **primary T cells** — different assay, depth, and normalization — so per-regulator effects must be z-scored/rank-standardized before concordance (this *is* the universal-vs-T-specific construction), and the join should go by **Ensembl ID** (`obs.gene_id`), not symbol. Per the agreed order, naming (Step 3) lands first; K562 is the next step, not this one.


## Cross-cell-type concordance: universal vs T-specific (Step 2)

Partition regulators by whether their downstream effect **agrees between a cancer line (K562) and primary CD4 T cells**: concordant = universal wiring, divergent = cell-type-specific regulation. This also gives an **independent** cross-validation of the Step-3 program names — K562 is external, the naming used only CD4, and the concordance uses only the effect *vectors*, not the labels. Reproduce: `python scripts/analyze_k562_concordance.py` → `docs/tables/k562_concordance.csv`, `docs/figures/36_k562_universal_vs_specific.png`.

**Data + joins (where signal leaks if done wrong).** The K562 side is Replogle-2022's **pre-aggregated** perturbation×gene Z-normalized bulk product (`K562_gwps_normalized_bulk_01.h5ad`, 375 MB — *not* the 8.8 GB cell-level file; the bulk *is* the per-regulator signature we need). Genes join by **Ensembl** (intersect → 6,740 shared); regulators join by Ensembl at the **gene level** — Replogle's promoter/guide rows collapsed by unweighted mean (the matrix has no NaN, so no imputation) and **602 non-targeting/control rows dropped**. CD4 side is Rest-preferred logFC (K562 is a resting proliferating line, so Rest is the apples-to-apples state). After the clean join, **6,407 regulators** survive (well above the ≥100 power gate). Concordance = per-regulator Pearson over co-measured genes on per-regulator z-scored vectors (0 = "no change" ≠ NaN = "not measured", so no impute-to-0).

**Four diagnostics — reported, not assumed.** (1) *Null*: permuting the regulator↔regulator correspondence across datasets centres at 0 (median 0.000, q95 0.038). (2) *Power* — the decisive control: `spearman(concordance, min_kd_rank) = 0.200`, where `min_kd_rank` is the min of the two **per-side KD percentile ranks** (raw KD magnitudes are not comparable across the two assays, so ranks; the exact column is shipped in the CSV and the 0.200 round-trips from it — the per-side ranks alone give 0.122 CD4 / 0.304 K562). A positive 0.200 means the **raw** split (1,182 universal / 1,556 T-specific) is partly a weak-KD artifact, mostly K562-side. (3) *Coverage*: `spearman(concordance, n_co_measured) = −0.010` — data is complete, missingness drives nothing. (4) *Donor-robust conditioning*: universal are 45% donor-robust vs T-specific 15%.

**Headline = the stratified, not the raw split.** Restricting to well-powered regulators (strong KD *both* sides) the split **survives**: **508 universal vs 227 T-specific** — 227 regulators diverge across cell types despite strong knockdown in both, i.e. real cell-type specificity, not power. Intersecting further with donor-robustness gives a conservative core of **201 universal / 49 T-specific**. This well-powered number is a **floor, not the total**: an immune regulator lowly expressed in K562 has weak K562 KD *because* it is T-specific, so the power control (which penalises it) removes real T-specific signal — the true specificity is larger than 227.

**The naming cross-validates — and the naive hypothesis inverts (which strengthens it).** We expected activation→T-specific; the data says the opposite, and it is biologically cleaner:
- **Factor 6's SAGA chromatin core → universal.** The clean claim rests on a **single well-powered anchor: SUPT20H** (concordance 0.186, well-powered + donor-robust). TADA2B/TAF6L also score universal but are *not* independently well-powered *and* their CD4 effect is partly cis-inflated (Step-1 audit), so they are consistent-with, not evidence of equal weight. Housekeeping transcription machinery working alike in any cell — as expected.
- **Factor 1's immune Rac→WAVE→actin module → T-specific.** Symmetrically, the clean claim rests on a **single well-powered anchor: DOCK2** (a lymphocyte Rac-GEF — T-specific, well-powered, donor-robust; survives every control). **NCKAP1L = Hem1** (the *hematopoietic* WRC paralog, −0.040), **AHR** (−0.029) and **ARNT** (−0.005) are donor-robust but **not** well-powered and their concordances sit essentially at zero — so their T-specific label is carried by the *conservative-floor* argument (an immune gene lowly expressed in K562 has weak K562 KD *because* it is T-specific), not by independent well-powered signal. This is exactly parallel to the SUPT20H↔TADA2B/TAF6L asymmetry on the universal side. (WASF2/WAVE2, the ubiquitously-expressed WRC subunit, sits intermediate — consistent: the *immune-specific* components diverge, the general one does not.) So the K562 comparison **independently recovers**, via DOCK2 cleanly and the rest under the floor, that the immune-specific cytoskeleton axis is T-cell-specific — two independent analyses (Step-3 naming + K562) converging on the same biology.

That a result contradicting the author's prior still comes out coherent is what makes it trustworthy; the split is defensible because the control we ran (power) found contamination and we led with the stratified, donor-robust core (and single well-powered anchors per arm) instead of the raw counts.


## RMT-clean regulator communities (Step 6 — core)

A third, independent read of the operator: instead of decomposing the tensor (CP factors, SVD programs), build the regulator–regulator correlation and find communities of co-acting regulators. Reproduce: `python scripts/operator_communities.py` → `docs/tables/operator_communities_3106.csv`, `operator_community_spectrum_3106.csv`, `docs/figures/37_operator_communities_3106.png` (needs `pip install leidenalg python-igraph`).

**Denoise before clustering.** The raw 3106×3106 Pearson correlation (regulator fingerprints = the 2000-gene z-score tensor with its 3 conditions concatenated → 3106×6000, aspect ratio q = N/T = 0.52) is dominated by two nuisance structures RMT isolates cleanly: a **global co-variation mode** (λ₀ = **167**, a huge outlier — the shared perturbation-response axis that loads every regulator) and a **Marchenko–Pastur noise bulk** (upper edge λ+ = **1.49**, noise variance σ² = 0.50 fit iteratively *after* deflating the global mode). Reconstructing the correlation from only the **336 signal eigenvalues** in (λ+, λ₀) isolates genuine co-regulation; the bulk tracks the theoretical MP density (Figure 37, left). Honest caveat: the 6000 columns are not i.i.d. (the same gene appears across 3 conditions), so the effective sample size < 6000 and **λ+ is a lower bound** on the true noise edge — some noise eigenvalues likely leak into the 336 "signal" count. The permutation null (2nd pass) is the guardrail.

**Communities.** kNN graph (k=15, positive signal-correlation edges) → Leiden (`RBConfigurationVertexPartition`) resolution sweep (γ 0.2–2.0) × 50 seeds = 500 partitions → consensus co-assignment matrix → communities gated by mean intra-community co-assignment stability. Of **8 communities, 3 clear the ≥ 0.8 stability gate** (sizes 177 / 132 / 87; s_c 0.835 / 0.834 / 0.869); the smallest stable module (n = 87, s_c 0.87) is notably **45% donor-robust** (vs ~10% in the other two). Biology sanity: the canonical SAGA subunits (TADA2B, TAF6L, SUPT20H, SGF29, SUPT7L, USP22) **all co-cluster into one community** ✓ — but that community sits at s_c = 0.56, *below* the gate, i.e. the SAGA core is embedded in a looser group rather than forming a tight stable module at this consensus resolution.

**Scope (honest).** This is the **core** pipeline only. What the stable communities *are* (complex/program enrichment), whether their structure exceeds an MP/permutation null, and whether they recover the CP-factor / SVD-program biology from a third independent method — the interpretive payoff — is the deliberate **2nd pass** (`op.hypergeometric_enrichment` vs SAGA/TCR/Mediator/IFN sets; column-permutation RMT null; overlap vs `operator_cp_factors_3106.csv` and the SVD programs). Until then these are a denoised, stability-gated grouping, not yet a validated biological claim.

### Step 6, 2nd pass — validation & identity

**Task A — noise edge recalibrated (guardrail).** The MP edge λ+ = 1.49 is a *lower bound* because the 6000 feature columns are not i.i.d. (same gene × 3 conditions). The **empirical** edge — top eigenvalue of 100 column-permuted null correlations, 95th percentile — is **λ+ = 2.95**, so the honest signal count is **92 eigenvalues, not 336**. This recalibrates the *rank*, not the communities: the consensus partition's modularity (Q = 0.506) sits **z = 259** above a label-permutation null, so the community structure is overwhelmingly real. (`operator_community_null_3106.csv`.)

**Task B — the SAGA module is cross-cell-type, over the module not one anchor (the result).** Step 2 established SUPT20H *alone* as the cis-clean, K562-universal SAGA anchor. Community detection groups the SAGA subunits (SUPT20H/SUPT7L/TADA2B/TAF6L/USP22/SGF29 in community 2; only KAT2B falls elsewhere), which lets us test the *module* (n > 1). In the shipped K562 concordance, **5 of the 6 shared subunits are universal** (SUPT20H 0.19, TADA2B 0.16, TAF6L 0.15, USP22 0.09, SUPT7L 0.09; KAT2B intermediate) — the program generalizes over the module, not a single anchor. Non-circular confirmation: rebuilding the regulator–regulator correlation **in K562** (Replogle bulk, same pipeline as CD4), the SAGA subunits form a coherent cluster — mean intra-correlation **0.244** vs **0.010 ± 0.014** for random same-size regulator sets (**z = 16.1, p < 0.0001**). The SAGA module re-forms as a module in a second cell type. (`k562_saga_module_3106.csv`.)

**Task C — identity (complete, CORUM).** Enrichment against the **438 CORUM complexes** with ≥2 members in the panel (of 2,734), BH-FDR per community. Community 2 (the SAGA group, s_c = 0.56, below the gate) re-confirms strongly: **SAGA complex GCN5-linked (FDR 3e-4, 8/8)**, STAGA SPT3-linked (1e-3, 8/9), SAGA (4e-3), Mediator (3e-2) — coactivator machinery. Of the three *stable* communities, **community 7 (n = 87, 45% donor-robust) is the Mitochondrial Respiratory Chain Complex I** (NADH dehydrogenase): **FDR 1.4e-7** (8/13 holoenzyme subunits), corroborated across seven Complex-I assembly/intermediate CORUM entries — a clean, high-confidence identity for exactly the module we most wanted to name. A mitochondrial-respiratory module surfacing *coherently under CD4 perturbation* is expected biology, not a housekeeping artifact: mitochondrial electron-transport-chain respiration is **induced and essential** for T-cell activation, proliferation and memory formation (Tarasenko et al., *Nat. Commun.* 2025, `10.1038/s41467-025-65910-w`), so knocking down Complex-I subunits produces a coordinated, activation-relevant transcriptional response — which is exactly why the subunits co-regulate into one stable, donor-robust community. Communities 5 and 6 (n 177/132) match **no annotated CORUM complex** at FDR < 0.05 — stable, reproducible regulator modules with no known-complex identity, itself a reportable result. (`operator_community_enrichment_3106.csv`; regenerate with `scripts/operator_community_identity.py --corum data/genesets/allComplexes.txt`.)

**Task D — convergence with CP (consistency).** The CP factors that carry SAGA — factors 2/3/5 — map to **community 2 at 100% concentration** (every top regulator in one community); the "mixed" gated factors 1/6 spread across communities, consistent with the Step-3 finding that they are multi-module. A third independent method agrees on the SAGA grouping. (`operator_community_cp_overlap_3106.csv`.)

**Net.** The community structure is real (modularity z = 259) but the honest signal-mode count is **92, not 336**. Two results carry: (1) the **cross-cell-type SAGA module** — 5 universal subunits plus z = 16 module cohesion recovered independently in K562 — an n > 1 upgrade of the single-anchor Step-2 claim; and (2) CORUM names the mystery stable module: **community 7 (45% donor-robust) is Mitochondrial Respiratory Chain Complex I (FDR 1.4e-7)**, while the other two stable communities correspond to no annotated complex — real modules the atlas has not otherwise surfaced.

### Step 6, 2nd pass · Task 3 — autoimmune-GWAS signal of the communities (matched null only)

Do the communities carry more autoimmune genetic-risk signal than chance? Answered against a **length-matched, MHC-robust null over the 3,106 T-expressed panel regulators** — never the genome (immune genes are long, SNP-dense, near the MHC; a genome null would be trivially "enriched"). GWAS = Open Targets `genetic_association` for SLE/RA/MS/Sjögren/T1D (score ≥ 0.01); community load = summed per-gene max genetic score; null = 10,000 resamples **matched by gene-length decile**, reported with and without the chr6 MHC block. This is a genetics-corollary **nomination**, not a causal claim; directionality is not asserted. (`community_gwas_matchednull_3106.csv`, `community_gwas_genes_3106.csv`.)

**The controls validate the null.** The metabolic **Complex-I community (comm 7) is NOT enriched** (fold 0.45, p = 0.98) — a leaky genome-naive null would falsely light it up; it does not. The **SAGA coactivator community (comm 2) IS enriched** (fold 1.35, **p = 0.0025**, surviving MHC exclusion at p = 0.0023), **consistently across four diseases** (RA/SLE/MS/T1D loads 15.1/8.4/8.3/9.7 — not a single-disease artifact), including canonical T-cell risk regulators **GATA3, CBLB, UBASH3A, IL12RB2, RASGRP3** — a rigorous, MHC-robust nomination that the coactivator module touches autoimmune risk genes.

**The stable unnamed communities carry NO disease signal above the matched null.** Community 5 (n = 177): fold 0.97, p = 0.55. Community 6 (n = 132): a suggestive but **non-significant** fold 1.31 (p = 0.10; RA-driven, p = 0.13 with MHC excluded). Neither clears p < 0.05 against the length-matched, MHC-excluded background. This is a **clean negative**: communities 5 and 6 are stable, reproducible regulator modules with *no* known-complex identity (CORUM) *and* *no* autoimmune genetic enrichment above a properly matched null — reported, not forced. That the controls behave correctly (SAGA positive, Complex I negative) makes the negative credible rather than a power artifact.

### Bloque C · MVP — Inductive Matrix Completion: can we predict a NEVER-SEEN regulator? (NULL)

The completion flagship (Step 3b) is **transductive**: it holds out a *condition fiber* of an otherwise-observed regulator. This closes gap #3 with the harder **inductive** question — predict the trans response of a regulator whose *every* condition is absent from training, from its features alone (`Y ≈ X W Zᵀ`; for a held-out regulator *i* the prediction `Xᵢ W Zᵀ` is computable without ever seeing a row of *i*). Test = **leave-REGULATOR-out**: 5-fold over all 3,106 regulators, each test regulator fully absent (all 3 conditions) from training; ridge = a full-rank IMC and a legitimate inductive baseline; per-regulator held-out R² measured against the *training* mean, so the mean-predictor is R² = 0 by construction. The design is **symmetric** — this is the one analysis that can come out worse than what we have, and a null is a clean, reportable result, not a hidden one. (`scripts/imc_inductive.py`, `imc_leave_regulator_out_3106.csv`, `imc_feature_matrix_3106.csv`.)

**Result — clean NULL.** From **605 regulator features** (5 numeric — length, STRING degree/weighted-degree, n_GO, n_Pfam; `is_TF`; 46 DBD-family one-hots; 500 GO + 53 Pfam multi-hots), the inductive held-out R² is **−0.0005** (median −0.0001; per-regulator 10th–90th percentile [−0.010, +0.009]) — indistinguishable from zero and marginally *below* the mean-predictor. It does **not** beat the two mandatory baselines: **b0 mean-predictor** (R² = 0 by construction) and **b1 features-shuffled** (R² = −0.00003; X rows permuted to de-align features from Y). Tellingly, RidgeCV selected genuinely *low* regularization on the real features (α ≈ 1–5×10³, interior of the grid — the model was *allowed* real feature influence and still found no generalizable structure), whereas on shuffled features it drove α to the ceiling (10⁵–10⁶), collapsing to the mean-predictor exactly as it should.

**Verdict (reported as-is, not re-run to positive).** Regulator features — sequence family, network degree, GO/Pfam annotation — do **not** carry enough signal to predict the trans response of a never-seen regulator on this dataset; inductive prediction of unseen perturbations remains **unresolved** here. This is a property of the data, not a model failure, and is consistent with genome-scale foundation models failing to clear the mean-predictor bar (Ahlmann-Eltze et al. 2025). The transductive operator (Step 3b, BCV rank-7, R² ≈ 0.06) is untouched: it predicts unseen *conditions* of *observed* regulators — a strictly easier, separately validated task, and the inductive R² being lower is expected and correct, not a shortfall. Per the pre-registered symmetric criterion, because ridge did not beat shuffled, the low-rank `X W Zᵀ` (ALS) extension is **not** pursued — the MVP ends on a documented negative.

#### Robustez no-lineal del nulo inductivo (CatBoost leave-regulator-out)

Ridge showed regulator features do not predict a never-seen regulator's trans response *linearly*. This robustness check asks whether a strong **non-linear** learner (CatBoost, gradient-boosted trees) recovers interaction signal ridge misses — framed as robustness of the null, **not** a second attempt at signal. Everything that defines the test is held identical to the ridge null for direct comparability: same `KFold(5, shuffle=True, random_state=0)` split over the same 3,106 regulators, same feature matrix X (`build_X` imported from `imc_inductive.py`), same per-regulator 6,000-gene R² metric vs the training mean, same shuffled-X control (b1). The one change that makes single-output CatBoost tractable and valid: reduce Y by a **train-fold-only** truncated SVD to K ∈ {10, 30} (bracketing the BCV predictive rank ~7 / signal ~92), fit one CatBoost per component (depth 6, 500 iters, early stopping on an internal train val split, seed 0, **no** hyperparameter tuning), then reconstruct the K predicted scores to 6,000-dim with the train SVD basis. (`scripts/imc_nonlinear.py`, `imc_nonlinear_catboost_3106.csv`.)

**Result — the null is reinforced.** Held-out R² is indistinguishable from zero at both K (K=10: −0.0003; K=30: −0.0007) and does **not** beat the shuffled-feature control (−0.0004 / −0.0006). A **capacity probe** closes the "model too weak" escape: unleashed (no early stopping), CatBoost fits the training scores to **R² = +0.133** while its held-out R² is **−0.022** — capacity is demonstrably present, generalizable signal is absent. This is the exact tree analogue of ridge driving its regularization to the ceiling: given the freedom to fit, both model classes find nothing that transfers to unseen regulators.

**Verdict.** A strong non-linear learner extracts no more inductive signal than the linear one — the limit is the **data**, not the model class. The inductive null (Bloque C) is robust: regulator features (sequence family, network degree, GO/Pfam annotation) do not predict the trans response of a never-seen regulator, linearly or non-linearly. Reported as-is; per the pre-registered symmetric criterion (real ≉ shuffled at any K), nothing is re-run with more iterations, depth, or features.
